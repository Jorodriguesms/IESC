About Us

We are the Intercultural Education for Social Change (IESC), an online platform for social educators, with the aim of disseminating social projects focused on education and communication strategies as alternatives that challenge traditional media models. Our initiative works in cooperation with several NGOs and social projects across the country that contribute to the diversity of works shared on our website.

Principles

Creation of a new media culture, with a critical media literacy in education; Developing a sense of social participation in political decisions and policies formulations about the matter; Decolonial an intercultural approach on solving educational problems with communication and media; Creating a transformative education, not only a reproduction of the hegemonic representations.

Objectives

Social Education

To think about social education and social pedagogy, critical interculturality becomes a tool to work with the epistemological challenges of Latin American decolonial study groups. We can think of a critical communication that does not reproduce cultural domination structures of large media corporations. The platform was developed to be a free and autonomous media, committed to social change. The idea is to democratize knowledge based on the dissemination of projects and strategies centered on the use of media for social education and of its own critical literacy.

Interculturality

Our platform is based on Vera Candau's concept of interculturality, more specifically a critical interculturality. The understanding that difference opposes equality brings normalizing discourses into teaching practice, it is seen as an anomaly, an issue to be resolved, since all students must be able to integrate the work society.

Epistemologies of the South

“We cannot treat southern theory as if it were a distinct set of propositions, an alternative paradigm to be erected in opposition to hegemonic concepts. We don't want another system of intellectual dominance.” (CONNELL, p. 15) With that in mind, this project objective is to challenge the usual models of knowledge by diversifying its own practices: “The argument for Southern theory is not primarily about different propositions, but about different knowledge practices. [...] challenging the usual models of “knowledge transfer” and the epistemic and institutional underpinnings of North/South divides. Encouraging such learning is the practical purpose of my narrative in Southern Theory.” (CONNELL. p. 17)

Contemporary Debates

Considering the contemporary discussion around media literacy, emphasizing the end of 2010’s decade, there are many concerns about the topics of Fake News, the use of social media by minors, the social and cultural impact caused by both the traditional and informal journalisms, the use of communication networks - sponsored by enterprises and controlled by the BigTechs - for political purposes with manipulative content, beyond others.

Public Policies

it is impossible to separate the media culture from the efforts of educational progress for a social change, neither for guaranteeing a critical thinking to the students, nor to the society in general. Therefore, it is important to establish a dialogue between social organizations, civil society, universities, and the State in order to press for public policies on the matter, with a decolonial thought being privileged.
